Phishing Awareness

Learn how to recognise and avoid phishing attacks.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyberattack where criminals disguise themselves as trusted entities to steal sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data. These attacks are usually carried out through fake websites, emails, or messages.

Common Phishing Indicators

  • Misspelled domain names (e.g. paypa1.com instead of paypal.com)
  • URLs with suspicious characters like @, -, or extra subdomains
  • Fake HTTPS — a padlock icon does not always mean a site is safe
  • Urgent or threatening language (e.g. "Your account will be suspended!")
  • Requests for sensitive information via email or unfamiliar websites
  • URL shorteners hiding the real destination (e.g. bit.ly links)
  • Suspicious top-level domains like .tk, .ml, .ga

Phishing vs Legitimate URLs

Legitimate URL Phishing URL
https://www.paypal.com/login http://paypal-secure-login.tk/verify
https://www.facebook.com http://facebook.login-verify.com
https://www.bankofamerica.com http://bankofamerica.account-update.ml

How to Stay Safe

  • Always verify the URL before entering any personal information
  • Use this tool to check suspicious URLs before clicking
  • Never click links in unsolicited emails or messages
  • Enable two-factor authentication on your accounts
  • Keep your browser and antivirus software up to date
  • When in doubt, go directly to the website by typing the URL yourself
